Before You Start
How to Use This Pack
These 5 workflows cover the full GTM funnel from first contact to closed deal. Run them in sequence or pick the one you need most right now.
The core idea
A solo founder doing GTM manually loses 15-20 hours per week on work that is structured, repeatable, and context-heavy - exactly the three conditions where Claude produces reliable, usable output. These workflows do not replace your judgment. They remove the manual execution around it.
1
Pick your entry point
All 5 prompts work in claude.ai (browser or app). For Claude Code users: save each prompt as a .md file in your project and run from terminal. For automation: drop into n8n or Make as a Claude node with a webhook trigger.
2
Replace all placeholders before running
Every prompt has values in [BRACKETS]. Replace them with your actual offer, ICP, and context. Generic input = generic output. The more specific you are, the more the prompt sounds like you wrote it yourself.
3
Run Workflows 01 and 02 together
Lead research (01) feeds directly into outbound personalization (02). Run 01 first, paste the output as context into 02. This is the core daily loop for a 1-person GTM operation.
4
Use Workflows 03-05 per deal, not per day
Discovery prep (03), proposal drafting (04), and follow-up to close (05) trigger on specific deal events - not on a daily schedule. Run them when the deal moment arrives.
5
Model recommendation: Claude Sonnet
Use Sonnet for all 5 workflows. Haiku works for Workflow 01 (research) if you are processing 50+ leads at volume. Opus is not necessary here - these are structured execution tasks, not open reasoning problems.

Workflow 01
Saves 45 min per lead vs manual research
Lead Research + ICP Qualification
Turn a company name and LinkedIn URL into a complete prospect brief. Pain hypothesis, buying signals, ICP score, and first message angle - before you write a single word of outreach.
Inputs
->Company name + LinkedIn URL
->Your ICP definition
->Your offer in 1-2 sentences
->Industry / size filter (optional)
Claude outputs
->ICP fit score (1-10) with reasoning
->Pain hypothesis (2-3 sentences)
->Buying signal flags
->Best outreach angle + hook idea
->Disqualify flag if not ICP fit
Ready-to-use prompt
WORKFLOW_01 · lead-research.md
ROLE: You are my GTM research analyst. Qualify this lead against my ICP and build a prospect brief. MY OFFER: - Product: [what you sell in 1 sentence] - ICP: [e.g. founders of 1-20 person B2B service businesses, US/UK] - Core pain solved: [specific pain, not generic] - Deal size target: [e.g. $1,500-5,000/month] LEAD INFO: - Company: [COMPANY NAME] - LinkedIn URL: [URL] - Contact: [Name, Title] - What I know about them: [anything - recent post, funding, job posting, mutual connection] OUTPUT: ## ICP FIT SCORE: [X/10] Reason: [2 sentences - why they do or do not fit] ## PAIN HYPOTHESIS [2-3 sentences: what problem they are most likely experiencing based on their role, company stage, and any signals available] ## BUYING SIGNALS [List: what signals suggest they are ready or receptive now] ## DISQUALIFY FLAG [YES/NO - if YES, explain why and suggest whether to skip or park for later] ## BEST OUTREACH ANGLE [1 paragraph: what angle to lead with, what to reference, what to avoid] ## HOOK IDEA [1-2 sentence opening line for a first message, not a full draft]
n8
n8n / Make automation
Trigger: new row in lead list (Airtable / Notion)
Claude node: run research prompt
Output: enrich same row with brief + score
Filter: only pass ICP score 7+ to workflow 02
Common mistakes
No context provided - Claude needs at least one signal beyond company name to produce a useful pain hypothesis
Running on every lead regardless of fit - use the disqualify flag to cut your list before outbound
Using the hook idea as the full message - it is a starting point, not a finished line
Workflow 02
Output: 20 personalized sequences per hour
Cold Outbound - First Message + Full Sequence
Feed in the brief from workflow 01 and get 3 message variants plus a full follow-up sequence. Angle changes per lead. No generic templates. No "I hope this finds you well."
Inputs
->Output from workflow 01 (pain brief + angle)
->Your offer + proof point
->CTA (call / reply / demo link)
->Tone rules (your writing style)
Claude outputs
->3 first-touch variants (different angle each)
->Day 3 follow-up (value add, not bump)
->Day 7 final bump (low pressure close)
->Subject lines for email variants
Ready-to-use prompt
WORKFLOW_02 · outbound-sequence.md
ROLE: You are my outbound specialist. Write a personalized cold outreach sequence for this lead. MY OFFER: - Product: [what you sell] - Proof point: [specific result: e.g. "founders using this close their first 3 clients in under 60 days"] - CTA: [e.g. 20-min call / reply with one word / book at link] LEAD BRIEF (from Workflow 01): [PASTE FULL OUTPUT FROM WORKFLOW 01 HERE] TONE RULES (non-negotiable): - No em dashes. No curly quotes. No "I hope this finds you well." - Short paragraphs. Under 80 words per message. First person. - Never mention AI, Claude, or automation in the message. - Sound like a founder, not a sales tool. - Direct. Specific. One clear ask per message. OUTPUT: ## VARIANT A - Direct angle Subject: [subject line] [Under 80 words. Lead with their specific pain. Reference one signal. CTA at end.] ## VARIANT B - Curiosity angle Subject: [subject line] [Under 80 words. Open with a question about their situation. No pitch until line 3.] ## VARIANT C - Social proof angle Subject: [subject line] [Under 80 words. Lead with a result for someone in a similar situation. Connect to them.] ## FOLLOW-UP DAY 3 [Under 50 words. New piece of insight or value. Not "just following up".] ## FOLLOW-UP DAY 7 - Final bump [Under 35 words. Easy yes or no. Low pressure. Genuine close-out tone.]
n8
Automation flow
Input: enriched lead row from workflow 01
Claude node: generate sequence
Output: draft sequences to Instantly / Lemlist
Review step: human approves before send
Common mistakes
Sending all 3 variants to the same lead - pick one per lead, A/B test variants across batches of 20+
Skipping the tone rules - without them Claude defaults to corporate language that kills response rates
Using day 3 follow-up as a bump - it must add a new observation or insight, not repeat the ask

Discovery Call Prep System
Walk into every call knowing exactly what to probe, what to validate, and what signals to watch for. Founders who prep this way close 2-3x faster because they diagnose instead of pitch.
Inputs
->Lead brief from workflow 01
->What they said in the reply that booked the call
->Your offer tiers + pricing
->Common objections you hear
Claude outputs
->5 discovery questions ranked by priority
->Pain signals to listen for
->Budget / timeline qualification logic
->Objection pre-emption notes
->Recommended offer tier to propose
Ready-to-use prompt
WORKFLOW_03 · call-prep.md
ROLE: You are my sales coach. Prepare me for a discovery call with this prospect. I need to diagnose, not pitch. MY OFFER: - Product: [what you sell] - Tier 1: [$X/month - what included] - Tier 2: [$Y/month - what included] - Common objections: [e.g. "too expensive", "not the right time", "already using X"] PROSPECT CONTEXT: [PASTE LEAD BRIEF FROM WORKFLOW 01] WHAT THEY SAID WHEN BOOKING: [paste their exact reply or booking form response] OUTPUT: ## CALL OBJECTIVE [Single sentence: what a successful call ends with] ## TOP 5 DISCOVERY QUESTIONS [Ranked. Each question: the question + why it matters + what answer signals strong fit] ## PAIN SIGNALS TO LISTEN FOR [List: if they say X, it means Y - specific trigger phrases and what they indicate] ## BUDGET + TIMELINE QUALIFICATION [How to ask without asking directly. What language signals budget readiness vs stalling.] ## OBJECTION PRE-EMPTION [For each common objection: how to address it before it comes up] ## RECOMMENDED TIER TO PROPOSE [Which tier fits based on available context + reasoning] ## ONE THING TO AVOID [The mistake most likely to lose this specific deal based on their profile]
CC
Claude Code setup
Save as call-prep.md in project root
Run 30 min before call: claude "prep call with [name]"
Output opens in terminal - review on second screen
Print or keep open during call as reference sheet
Common mistakes
Running this without the Workflow 01 brief - generic prep is useless; the value is in the lead-specific diagnosis
Reading questions from the list - use them as anchors, not scripts; the call must feel like a conversation
Skipping the "one thing to avoid" section - this is often the most valuable output in the whole prep

Ready-to-use prompt
WORKFLOW_04 · proposal-draft.md
ROLE: You are my proposal writer. Turn discovery notes into a structured commercial proposal draft. MY BUSINESS: - Company: [YOUR COMPANY] - Core offer: [what you sell in 1 sentence] - Tier 1: [$X - deliverables] | Tier 2: [$Y - deliverables] - Standard timeline: [e.g. 4-week engagement] CLIENT: - Company: [NAME] | Contact: [Name, Title] - Budget signal: [what they said or implied] - Decision timeline: [when they want to start] - Key pain (their exact words if possible): [paste quote] CALL NOTES: [PASTE YOUR RAW DISCOVERY NOTES HERE - messy is fine] OUTPUT: ## ENGAGEMENT SUMMARY [3-4 sentences restating their problem in their language. Must feel exactly right to them.] ## PROPOSED SCOPE [Numbered deliverables with 1-line description each] ## WHAT IS NOT INCLUDED [Bullet exclusions - scope protection against future creep] ## TIMELINE [Week-by-week or phase breakdown] ## INVESTMENT [Recommended tier + pricing rationale in 2 paragraphs. No apology for the price.] ## ASSUMPTIONS [What must be true for this scope to hold] ## NEXT STEP EMAIL Subject: Proposal - [Client] x [Your Company] [Short, direct email. Context, link placeholder, one CTA.]
CC
Claude Code setup
Save call notes as notes-[client].txt immediately after call
Run: claude "draft proposal from notes-[client].txt"
Output: proposal-[client].md - review and paste into doc
Send within 2 hours of call for best conversion rate
Common mistakes
Using your language instead of theirs - paste their exact words; proposals convert better when clients recognize their own problem described back to them
Skipping exclusions - what is out of scope matters as much as what is in scope
Sending without editing the investment section - read it before sending; Claude does not know your relationship dynamics or how they reacted to price on the call
Workflow 05
Runs from proposal sent to signed deal
Follow-Up to Close Sequence
Most deals die in silence after the proposal. Not from rejection - from friction, distraction, and no clear next step. This workflow keeps momentum without being pushy.
Inputs
->Proposal sent date + deal size
->What they said at end of call
->Decision timeline they gave
->Any objection or hesitation signals
Claude outputs
->Day 2: value-add check-in (not a nudge)
->Day 5: question that surfaces the real objection
->Day 9: social proof or case angle
->Day 14: final close or park message
->Objection response drafts if known
Ready-to-use prompt
WORKFLOW_05 · follow-up-to-close.md
ROLE: You are my deal closer. Write a follow-up sequence for a proposal that has been sent but not yet signed. DEAL CONTEXT: - Client: [Name, Company] - Deal size: [$X/month or $Y total] - Proposal sent: [DATE] - Their decision timeline: [what they said on the call] - How the call ended: [positive / hesitant / needed to check with partner / etc] - Known objection or concern: [if any - e.g. "price is a stretch", "timing not ideal"] TONE RULES: - Short. Direct. Never "just checking in." - Each message adds something new: an insight, a question, a relevant example. - No pressure. The goal is to remove friction, not push. - Sound like a founder, not a salesperson. OUTPUT: ## DAY 2 - Value add check-in [Under 60 words. Add one relevant insight, resource, or observation connected to their specific situation. Soft CTA: does this land?] ## DAY 5 - Surface the real objection [Under 50 words. Ask the one question that gets them to say what is actually holding them back. Specific to their situation.] ## DAY 9 - Social proof or case angle [Under 60 words. Reference a similar founder or situation. Not a testimonial - a parallel that makes them see themselves in it.] ## DAY 14 - Final close or park [Under 40 words. Either close or give them an easy way to say not now without burning the relationship.] ## OBJECTION RESPONSE DRAFTS [If a known objection was provided: write 2-sentence response that acknowledges without caving]
n8
Automation flow
Trigger: proposal status = "sent" in CRM or Notion
Claude node: generate sequence on day of send
Scheduled sends: Gmail drafts at day 2, 5, 9, 14
Stop trigger: deal status changes to "signed" or "lost"
Common mistakes
Sending day 2 as a nudge - it must add value; "just wanted to follow up" kills deals, it does not close them
Skipping day 5 - surfacing the real objection early is the single highest-leverage step in this sequence
Waiting until day 14 to send anything - deals that go cold at day 7 rarely warm back up; stay present early
